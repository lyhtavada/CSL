## 1. Getting Started & Setup

### Q1: How do I launch my loyalty program / can your team set it up and review it before I go live? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~14 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Core steps: (1) pick one goal first (retention, AOV, or referrals); (2) set your earning + redeeming rules under **Joy Admin → Reward programs**; (3) build a loyalty page via **Online Store → Themes → Customize → Add section → Joy Loyalty**; (4) enable the storefront widget in **Theme Editor → App embeds → Joy Loyalty - Widget → ON → Save**; (5) promote it via your menu, email, and social. A solid starter setup: a sign-up bonus, 1 point per $1, and a 100-point = small-discount reward. Loyalty needs **Shopify Admin → Settings → Customer accounts** enabled so customers can sign up and see their balance. We also offer a **free setup service** — share collaborator access and our team will style your Unified widget / loyalty page to match your branding and send you a preview before anything goes live. Desktop is recommended for setup; the onboarding flow can be hard to scroll on mobile.

### Q2: Can I rename "Points" or the program, or add a "PT" label to the numbers? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~5 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Yes. Rename the points currency at **Joy Admin → Settings → General → Custom point label** (enter singular/plural, e.g. "PT", "Beans", and choose whether the label shows before or after the number). To rename the program itself, edit the program name in **Reward programs**. If the custom label shows in some places but not on the redemption cards, send us the page where it's missing and we'll check it. You can also set a custom discount-code prefix (must follow the `ABCD-` pattern) under **Settings → General**.

### Q3: How do I preview the app / widget on my live store, and will enabling it affect my existing content? 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~4 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Preview the widget from **Joy Admin → On-site content → Widget → (Unified or Classic) → Setup**, then click the preview button. To make it appear on your live store you must enable the app embed: **Online Store → Themes → Customize → App embeds → Joy Loyalty - Widget → ON → Save**. Enabling the embed only adds the loyalty widget; it does not change or remove any of your existing theme content. The Unified widget template is available on all plans, including Free.

## 2. Testing & Going Live (Sandbox / Test mode)

### Q4: How do I test or preview the program without making it public or emailing customers? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~7 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Use Joy's built-in **Sandbox mode**: only the accounts on your test list earn points, so nothing goes public and no emails reach real customers.
1. **Joy Admin → Reward programs → Sandbox mode → Turn on**.
2. Click **Test list** (top-right) and add the email(s) you want to test with.
3. Sign in with a test account on your store and perform the actions (place an order, redeem, etc.).
4. Confirm results under **Joy Admin → Customers → [account] → Activities**.
5. Turn **Sandbox off** to go live.
Notes from this week: the test account must be **exactly** one of the whitelisted emails, or the widget shows "No activity yet." Sandbox accounts earn real points, so the Thank-You-page reward celebration reflects actual earned points too (a Thank-You vs widget point mismatch was found and fixed for one store). Always re-confirm the Sandbox state before and after edits.

### Q5: Does the program still track and let customers redeem points on the Free/Starter plan? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~5 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Yes. Customers still earn, hold, and redeem points on Free/Starter — the plan mainly changes your order quota and which advanced features (VIP Tiers, Milestones, POS, Rule engine, cart/checkout redemption) are available, not whether points work. On Free you get one Place-order earning rule; for more rules you'd move to the Rule engine on Advanced. You can upgrade at any time when you cross your plan's monthly order quota.

## 3. Points — Earning

### Q6: Why didn't a customer earn points for their order? (guest vs member, POS, "order recorded" status) 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~16 sessions
**Standard answer:**
The most common causes:
- **Customer was a guest at checkout.** Points are tied to a customer account. If the activity log shows "**order recorded**" instead of "placed order," the buyer was a guest at that moment and isn't eligible — they earn once they create/log into an account with the same email.
- **Order status trigger.** Points fire when the order reaches the status you set at **Joy Admin → Settings → Order → Reward by order status** (Paid or Fulfilled). A paid-but-unfulfilled (or COD/unpaid) order won't earn yet if you require Fulfilled.
- **Sales channel.** For POS orders, check **Joy Admin → Reward programs → Place order → Sales channels** and confirm POS is ticked.
- **Order-tag or other "Check if" conditions** on the rule weren't met (e.g. a 2x promo limited by Order tag). Remove the condition under the program's **Check if** section to apply to all members.
- **The earning program was inactive** at the time of checkout.
For partial/deposit (pre-order) orders, Joy awards the full points once on the moment the order becomes **Paid** — it never splits points across installments. For missing points on a valid case, share the order number + customer email and we'll investigate or add the points manually.

### Q7: Sign-up / welcome bonus points suddenly stopped being awarded. 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~6 sessions (escalation theme)
**Standard answer:**
There was a platform change to how the welcome reward triggers. Previously welcome points were granted automatically whenever any Shopify customer account was created (the `customers/create` event); that was changed to prevent over-awarding. Now the customer must **actually register / create an account on your store** to receive sign-up points — and they should receive them on their next login if the account was created without them. Two situations that no longer auto-award:
- **POS-created profiles** (no email / no real account) — sign-up points aren't granted; we can add them manually on request.
- **Shopify-auto-created profiles** (e.g. an email captured by a form/popup without the customer completing "Create account").
If a genuine registered customer still isn't getting the welcome bonus, share the emails and we'll escalate. For sign-up-form flows, you can also add points manually per customer at **Joy Admin → Customers → [customer] → adjust points**.

### Q8: Points were awarded, but with a long delay (sometimes 40–90 minutes), especially for in-store POS. 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~3 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Points are triggered by a webhook Shopify sends to Joy when the order reaches your configured status. In some cases that webhook is delayed on Shopify's side, so points land later than expected — a real pain for POS, where the customer is standing at the counter trying to redeem immediately. If you see a delay, share the order number, the order time, and when points actually posted, and we'll investigate. This has been logged as **product feedback** for the POS real-time case; for an isolated delay we ask you to monitor the next orders and flag any repeat.

### Q9: Can I earn/redeem points only on full-price products and exclude a specific product or sale items? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~4 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Yes — scope the rules to a **collection**. Put the items you want to exclude (a limited drop, sale items) in their own collection, then set the **Place order** earning rule's product condition to exclude it. To also block redeeming on those items, apply the same collection exclusion on the redeeming program. Notes from this week: (a) a product must be **Active** in Shopify (not "Unlisted/Draft") for Joy's search to find it; (b) for redeem-side exclusion past ~100 individual products, use a collection rather than picking products one by one; (c) a **VIP-tier order-level discount cannot exclude a single product** — order-level discounts apply to the whole order total. If you need per-product exclusion of a tier perk, that's a Shopify discount-system limitation we log as feedback.

### Q10: Can different VIP tiers earn at different rates, and can the multiplier be a decimal (1.25x / 1.5x)? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~5 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Per-tier earning rates are on **Advanced/Ultimate**. Set them at **Joy Admin → Membership → VIP Tiers → [tier] → Tier privileges → Add perks → Bonus Points**, then **Launch**. Bronze/base tier needs no perk — it earns at the base rate. Important: if you previously set tier multipliers under **Reward programs → Purchase reward (Rule engine)**, disable those old rules to avoid double-counting. Limitation surfaced this week: the Bonus-Points perk takes **whole** multipliers (2x, 3x), not decimals like 1.25x or 1.5x. Merchants needing fractional rates have been bridging it with **Shopify Flow**; we've logged decimal multipliers as product feedback.

### Q11: Daily-visit / Google-review / social points aren't being awarded. 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~4 sessions
**Standard answer:**
- **Google reviews** require **admin approval** before points are granted: the customer submits the review link in the widget, then you approve it at **Joy Admin → Customers → Activities → Google review**. You can also enable **automatic approval** in settings so points are granted on submission (we can switch this on for you).
- **Review-app points (Judge.me, Trustpilot, etc.):** points are auto-granted on **published** reviews, but Joy keeps its own approval status — reviews stuck "pending" in Joy won't pay out even if published in the review app. Set the default review status to "Approved" and confirm star/character conditions (e.g. 3★+, minimum-length). Character-limit and other advanced review conditions live in the **Rule engine** (Advanced) and can't be edited from the legacy editor.
- **Visit / daily visit / social:** the customer must be logged in when they perform the action.
If an action is configured and a logged-in customer still earns nothing, send the email + activity and we'll investigate.

### Q12: Can I cap a customer's total points balance, limit points earned per period, or delay points (pending points)? 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
- **Hard cap on total balance** (e.g. never exceed 500 points lifetime): not supported — logged as product feedback.
- **Limit points earned per period:** use **Joy Admin → Settings → Additional features → Limit customer earning** (Advanced) to set a max points a customer can earn within a timeframe. You can also set a **maximum points per order** on the Place-order program.
- **Pending points:** **Settings → Additional features → Pending points** (Advanced) holds earned points for a set period before they become spendable — useful for return windows or "available 18 days after delivery" type rules.

## 4. Points — Redemption & Discount Codes

### Q13: A customer can't redeem / a Joy code is invalid or won't apply at checkout. 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~9 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Joy redemptions create standard Shopify one-time-use codes tied to that customer. If a code is rejected, check: it isn't already used/expired, the cart meets the rule's conditions (minimum spend, eligible products/collections), and the customer is redeeming from their own logged-in account. A frequent cause this week: the redeeming program is set to combine only with **Order discounts**, so if the cart already has a **product** or **shipping** discount (or a wholesale/B2B app code), the Joy code won't apply — enable the matching discount class under the program's **Combinations** setting (and make sure the other discount allows combining). For an erroring code on a valid cart, share the customer email + code and we'll escalate.

### Q14: How do customers redeem — in the widget, cart, or at checkout — and can they redeem all their points at once? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~7 sessions
**Standard answer:**
The standard flow is: redeem in the **widget** (or loyalty page), which generates a one-time code that's applied at checkout. Notes:
- **Redeem at the Shopify checkout page** requires **Joy Ultimate + Shopify Plus (Checkout Extensibility)**. What many merchants call "redeem at checkout" is actually the **cart page / cart drawer** redemption (Redeem in line), available on **Advanced+**.
- **Redeem-in-line / cart drawer** binds to Shopify's **native** cart drawer selectors; it won't auto-work with 3rd-party slide-cart apps (e.g. qikify) or some page builders. For those, our team can add the matching **CSS selector** for your theme's cart button.
- **Redeem all points at once:** set the program to **Dynamic discount** and clear/raise the **Maximum points per redemption** field (this field only appears in Dynamic mode). Leaving the limit unchecked lets customers redeem their full balance.
- When you redeem on a customer's behalf from **Joy Admin → Customers → [customer] → Redeem**, the points are deducted immediately and you must send the generated code to the customer to apply at checkout.

### Q15: Customers can't tell a coupon was already used, and the revoke window is too short. 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~3 sessions
**Standard answer:**
On the **Unified** widget, used coupons move to an **Unavailable** section with a "used" badge, which the older Classic/v3 widget doesn't show clearly — switching to the Unified widget fixes the confusion (we can do this for you with a preview first). For **self-revoke**, enable it at **Joy Admin → Settings → Additional features → Enable customer self-revoke coupon**; revoking returns the spent points and disables the unused code. The **revoke time limit** (e.g. 48 hours) is configurable — if customers hit "TIME_LIMIT_EXCEEDED," increase the window in that same setting. Note the Loyalty-hub on the Customer Account page may still show coupon status less clearly; that's been logged for improvement.

### Q16: Why is a discount code with a wrong prefix (e.g. "JOY-…") being generated instead of my custom prefix? 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Joy applies the custom code prefix set at **Settings → General**. If some codes still come out as `JOY-…` (or a birthday voucher isn't using your "Birthday-" prefix), it's usually intermittent and tied to a specific time window or program. Send us a sample customer email + the affected code and grant **Discount** access in Shopify so we can trace when the prefix wasn't applied; we escalate prefix-mismatch cases to the tech team. Separately, redeemed coupons stay valid **after** their source program is deactivated — to invalidate them, revoke from **Customers → [customer] → Revoke**.

### Q17: A customer returned an order — are their redeemed points refunded automatically? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Only if you've enabled it. Turn on **"Refund points when customers' orders are refunded or cancelled"** in the redeeming program so spent points are returned automatically on refund/cancel going forward. For a past case where it wasn't enabled, you can manually re-add the points at **Joy Admin → Customers → [customer]**, then switch the setting on for future orders.

## 5. Migration & Importing / Exporting Data

### Q18: How do I migrate from another loyalty app or sync points between two of my stores? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~9 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Two paths: **Import** (self-serve, **Joy Admin → Customers → Import** CSV — points balance + birthday) and **assisted Migrate** (full data incl. VIP tier, status, phone). For a CSV, provide customer **email + point balance** (and tier if needed); birthdays use a `Birthday(MM/DD)` column. Import options let you **add to existing balances**, **reset balances**, or **update birthdays** — pick "add" vs "reset" carefully so you don't overwrite existing points. The file must be **CSV** (save Excel as CSV first). Customers must already exist in Shopify (matched by email). For store-to-store sync, export from the source store and import into the target with the right "add/reset" choice; large files take a little time and the search list can lag a couple of hours after a mass import even when the detail pages are already correct. For Appstle/Smile/etc., we recommend importing and verifying everything **before** switching live.

### Q19: How do I export my Joy customers / points — the Export button is missing? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~9 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Export from **Joy Admin → Customers → Export**. If you **don't see the Export button**, it has to be enabled on the backend first — ask support, then refresh the page and it appears (this is the most common reason "there's no export option"). By default the export may not include the **VIP tier** column; we can add it. For a **single customer's points history**, filter that customer's email in the activity export. Full programmatic API export is an Ultimate feature; for large customer files, share a Drive/Dropbox link rather than chat.

### Q20: Can I get a monthly report with Customer Name, Order ID, and Points Earned (for accounting)? 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
The dashboard doesn't offer a self-serve export that combines **Customer Name + Order ID + Points Earned** in one file, but our team can generate it for you on request — each line maps the customer to the order ID and points earned, which works for monthly expense/accounting reporting. Tell us the period and we'll prepare the file; the native **Activities** log covers point-changing events if you want a self-serve view in the meantime.

## 6. VIP Tiers

### Q21: How do I set up VIP tiers, perks (free shipping / % off / entry rewards), and keep them from re-counting? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~7 sessions
**Standard answer:**
VIP Tiers are on **Advanced/Ultimate** at **Joy Admin → Membership → VIP Tiers**. Choose a calculation rule (Points earned / Amount spent / Number of orders). For a **one-time** reward on reaching a tier use **Entry reward**; for an **ongoing** perk on every qualifying order (free shipping, standing % off, extra earning rate) use **Tier privileges → Add perks**. Always **Launch** after editing — a perk "not appearing" is usually an un-relaunched tier or a reward placed in the wrong section. Tier **demotion / "inactive for X months"** is tied to **purchases**, not general activity (reviews/visits don't count) — the on-screen wording can be misleading and has been flagged.

### Q22: A customer was manually assigned a tier but gets downgraded after a new order. 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~3 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Standard tiers recalculate on new orders, which can demote a manually-set customer. Use an **Exclusive tier** (Advanced/Ultimate) for protected groups (ambassadors, wholesale, "keep at top tier"): exclusive tiers are **manual-only** and aren't touched by automatic recalculation. Create it under **Membership → New tier → Exclusive tier (Manual only)**, then assign via **Joy Admin → Customers → [name] → Assign to exclusive tier**. If you want spending not to demote points-based tiers in general, base tiers on **Amount spent** (cumulative spend isn't reduced by redemptions). Share the affected customer email and we can enable/configure the exclusive tier.

### Q23: A customer got the same points/tier reward twice, or shows a negative "amount spent." 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~3 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Duplicate points/tier credit on a single order is a data issue — send the customer email and order and we'll deduct the duplicate, recalculate the tier, and run a scan to check whether other customers were affected (a duplicate-order bug from last year was already patched, with an automated re-scan available). A negative "amount spent" usually traces back to the same double-count. We escalate these to the tech team and remediate the affected records.

## 7. Referral

### Q24: How does the referral program work — caps, when the referrer is paid, and why edits/links don't seem to work? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~6 sessions
**Standard answer:**
The friend must use the **referral link** and a **new** email/account; the **referrer is rewarded only after the friend completes a qualifying order** (the activity stays "pending" until then). Set caps under the referral program's **Redemption limit** (program total + per-customer). Common issues this week:
- **Changes not saving** (referrer/referee reward stuck at the old value): an intermittent save bug — we've fixed it on affected stores; if it persists, tell us the store URL.
- **"Test mode" / shows code only:** the program is in Sandbox, or you're testing on the same browser logged into your own store — test in incognito or a different account.
- **"Shop now" redirect goes to the homepage instead of a product:** set the referral pop-up's redirect/product link correctly; we can adjust it for you.
You can also disable the referrer reward if you only want to reward the friend. Referral limits scale by plan (Starter 30 / Essential 100 / Advanced+ Unlimited).

## 8. Store Credit vs Points

### Q25: How does store credit work — earning rate, rounding/decimals, and why didn't my test order credit? 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~3 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Store credit grants a real cash-value balance on the customer's Shopify account, applied **automatically at checkout** as a payment method (no codes), powered by Shopify's native store credit. To enable it, select **Store Credit** as the reward type and, the first time, click **Grant Access → Confirm** to authorize Joy. Key behaviors merchants asked about this week:
- Joy calculates rewards on the order **subtotal** (shipping and tax are excluded); excluding gift cards needs an **Advanced** condition.
- Joy **simplifies the rate** by default (e.g. 2 credits/$100 → 1 credit/$50); the "don't simplify" checkbox controls display, not the actual rate.
- **Rounding** matters: with "Nearest," a small order (e.g. 2% on $20 = $0.40) can round to $0. We can enable **decimal store credit** so fractional amounts ($0.40, $1.60) are issued exactly.
- A per-$1 fractional rate (e.g. $0.02/$1) isn't configurable in the UI — logged for the tech team.
- Different store-credit rates **per VIP tier** are supported.
If a test order didn't credit, the usual cause is it didn't reach the configured status (Paid/Fulfilled) or didn't meet the minimum spend. "Failed to adjust store credit" = re-grant access.

## 9. Customer Accounts, Sync, Duplicates & Login

### Q26: A customer isn't showing in Joy / hasn't synced. 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~3 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Customers who **haven't placed an order yet** (or who only had an email captured without creating an account) may not have synced into Joy automatically. Customers who exist in **Shopify** should sync to Joy; if a Shopify customer is missing from **Joy Admin → Customers**, send us the email and we'll re-run the customer sync from Shopify and investigate. Note: a contact that lives only in Klaviyo (and isn't created in Shopify) will **not** appear in Joy.

### Q27: Customers can't log in to the loyalty widget ("couldn't sign in" / 6-digit code fails). 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
With New Customer Accounts, login uses Shopify's email + one-time-code flow (no traditional password). If the 6-digit code consistently fails, first try a stronger/different connection and logging in via the storefront account icon directly; if it still fails across devices, share the store URL and we'll have the tech team check the authentication/integration on your store. For POS-created customers accessing online, they're recognized by the same email once Shopify identifies them.

### Q28: A customer has duplicate accounts / points split across two profiles — can you merge them? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
This happens when a shopper checks out under two emails or across channels. Send both customer emails and the surviving account; support can merge the profiles and consolidate the balance (merging the customers natively in Shopify also combines the points). Encourage customers to log in before checkout to avoid splits.

## 10. Notifications & Privacy

### Q29: Joy is emailing customers who never enrolled or never completed an order — including privacy complaints. 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~3 sessions
**Standard answer:**
We take this seriously. The newsletter/sign-up reward email is triggered by the customer's **subscription status in Shopify**, independent of whether they logged in — so a contact synced into Shopify (even from a form/popup) can receive it, which is why some recipients say they "never signed up." To limit who gets emails: turn off the specific notification at **Joy Admin → Settings → Notifications** (there's a master global toggle plus per-program toggles), and **exclude** unwanted groups via **Settings → Pre-launch → Exclude segments** (tag them in Shopify, then exclude that segment — they won't earn, get the widget, or receive emails). Contacts that live only in Klaviyo and never sync to Shopify won't be emailed by Joy. For a GDPR/privacy concern we escalate to confirm exactly what was sent and ensure correct data handling. Best practice: keep notifications off while testing, then enable selectively at launch.

### Q30: How do I resend a redemption/coupon notification email, or fix test emails landing in spam? 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
To resend a redemption email with the coupon code to a specific customer, our team can resend it from the app — share the customer email. For your own **test emails** going to spam: check Spam/Promotions first; if you've configured **SMTP**, confirm it shows **Verified** at **Joy Admin → Settings → Email** so sending routes through your domain. For custom sender name/logo on emails (Essential+), we can update or remove it; on Free the email shows your store name / the default Joy sender.

## 11. Transaction / Order Limits

### Q31: I hit a transaction limit but have few orders — what is it and can I get unlimited? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~7 sessions
**Standard answer:**
A **transaction in Joy isn't a Shopify order** — 1 transaction = 1 loyalty activity (earning, redeeming, referral, etc.), so one order can create several transactions and the count climbs faster than your order count. A manual customer/points **import** also consumes transactions, which is a common reason new stores hit the cap early. If you've hit the transaction limit while still inside your plan's order quota, support can enable **Unlimited transactions** for verified stores — share your store URL and we'll clear the warning (refresh the app homepage afterward to confirm it's gone).

## 12. Pricing, Plans, Billing & Refunds

### Q32: How much does Joy cost, what's grandfathered, and why doesn't my bill match the plan price? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~10 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Starter $0 (250 orders/mo), Essential $29/mo (500 orders), Advanced $129/mo (2,000 orders), Ultimate $499/mo (7,000 orders), with per-100 overage on paid plans (Essential $15/100, Advanced $10/100, Ultimate $5/100). Merchants who installed before the Feb 2026 pricing keep **grandfathered** rates (e.g. Advanced at **$99**). A bill higher than the base price means **overage**: base plan + (extra orders ÷ 100 × overage rate). Example: Essential + 1,518 orders = $39.99 + (518/100 × $15) = $117.69 — so high-order stores save by moving up a tier. Free trials: 14 days Essential/Advanced, 30 days Ultimate (once per store). VIP Tiers, Milestones, Rule engine, POS and cart-drawer redemption start at **Advanced**; checkout-page redemption/API/Hydrogen are **Ultimate**. Mid-cycle upgrades are **prorated** by Shopify (unused time credited against the new charge), so you won't pay both plans. CS isn't authorized to change a merchant's plan (it needs payment-method approval) — we show where to upgrade. A special discount from an account manager (e.g. "30% off 3 months") applies **automatically** on the Shopify charge-approval page after upgrading — no code to paste.

### Q33: Can I pause, cancel, or refund without losing my data? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~6 sessions
**Standard answer:**
To stop charges without deleting your setup, **downgrade to Free/Starter** (Joy → Settings → Subscription → View all plans → Downgrade to Starter, or via Shopify Billing) — configuration and customers' points are retained, and you can reactivate later. To also stop new earning, turn off the earning programs. For a **refund**, Joy has a **30-day refund policy after upgrading** (refunds are not available once a store has had multiple billing cycles / past the 30-day window). ## 15. Integrations

### Q37: Does Joy integrate with Klaviyo / Omnisend / review apps / Shopify Flow / subscriptions / POS — and can it send bulk one-time emails? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~6 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Yes for **email marketing** (Klaviyo, Omnisend, Mailchimp, Drip, Sendlane — connect under **Joy Admin → Integrations**; the integrations are free, the email tools have their own pricing). Joy's **built-in emails are event-triggered notifications, not bulk campaigns** — for a one-time blast (e.g. "here's your points balance, come redeem"), build the segment in a connected email tool. **Review apps** (Judge.me etc.) award points for reviews (Essential+). **Shopify Flow** (Essential+) powers advanced/automation triggers. **Subscriptions:** renewal orders need a subscription app installed; for Joy Subscription it auto-connects (check **Integrations → Connected**). **POS** (Advanced+) lets customers earn/redeem in-store. If you already use Omnisend/Klaviyo for emails you don't need Joy's notifications on as well. Note: Klaviyo popup sign-ups don't sync directly into Joy — Joy syncs members from **Shopify**, so a contact must exist in Shopify to become a member.

### Q38: My ReCharge subscription discount codes stopped syncing. 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~1 session
**Standard answer:**
Joy's ReCharge integration no longer uses an API-key connection — it now detects ReCharge subscription orders from Shopify automatically, so there's nothing to "connect" on the Joy side. A system change that deprecated the old API connection briefly disrupted **discount-code syncing to ReCharge**; that has been restored and patched. If a Joy code is created on Shopify but not on ReCharge, send us the code and order and we'll verify the sync on your store.

### Q39: Can I auto-reward via Shopify Flow, use metafields for custom rewards, or read loyalty data in Liquid? 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
- **Metafields:** enable **Joy Admin → Settings → General → Show metafields on Shopify admin** (and "Allow third-party app access" + "Sync metafields data") so Joy writes Point Balance / VIP Tier to the Shopify customer record for apps like Klaviyo to read. A separate **"Sync Shopify metafields to Joy"** toggle pulls existing Shopify customer metafields (e.g. birthday) into Joy.
- **Custom rewards from arbitrary metafields:** Joy's programs mainly consume fields tied to built-in actions (like birthday); for fully custom conditions, bridge with **Shopify Flow** — share your rule and we'll suggest the closest setup.
- **Survey rewards** need a survey app with a native Shopify Flow connector (Zigpoll, SEA Survey); plain Google Forms can't auto-trigger points.
- **Customer Hub / C:Hub:** the Joy integration can show a blank loyalty panel when the dynamic button is clicked — share access and we'll have the tech team fix the binding (logged where unresolved).

## 16. Birthday Rewards

### Q40: How do birthday rewards work, and is "Complete birthday info" separate? 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Set up **Earning programs → Celebrate a birthday** with **Automatic** delivery and **Calendar month of birthday**, then turn the toggle **on** and save. The customer's birthday must be saved **at least ~30 days before** the birthday month for it to fire that year — a birthday added later only works next cycle (POS can't capture it; customers add it in their Joy profile, or you can sync it from a Shopify metafield). The **"Complete birthday info"** reward is a separate, optional incentive that gives bonus points when a customer first enters their birthday — you don't need it enabled for the monthly birthday reward to work. If birthday discount codes "don't work," check the coupon's **expiry window** (e.g. 7 days) — the customer may be applying it after it expired.

## 17. Analytics

### Q41: My analytics numbers don't reconcile / I can't find repeat-purchase by tier. 🆕 new
**Frequency:** ~3 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Different views measure different things — "New members" counts the selected date range while the "Loyalty Members" scorecard is all-time, and the line chart applies its own filter, so small gaps are expected; large gaps we investigate (the analytics feature was being revamped, so some Member Analytics figures could be temporarily unstable). The **Overview** reflects your own store's data (members/revenue/redemption rate), while the **Feature Requests** panel shows requests across all merchants. There's no native **repeat-purchase-rate by VIP tier** report yet — as a workaround, the "Returning customers – Joy active redeemers" metric opens in **Shopify Analytics**, and enabling auto-tagging of active redeemers (Settings → Additional features) gives you data to segment; full per-tier repeat-rate is logged as feedback.

## 18. Bug reports, performance & escalations (misc)

### Q42: Joy is causing a 404 / blank section / console or performance issue on my store. 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~5 sessions
**Standard answer:**
For storefront errors (404 on a Joy admin page, a blank VIP-tier table, a section rendering only its header, duplicate-program editor opening blank, an image that won't delete): many clear with a **cache clear / different browser / page reload**, or are a **display glitch** that refreshes away. A blank VIP-Tier table or empty loyalty block is usually the **app embed being disabled** in the theme — re-enable it (Theme Editor → App embeds → Joy Loyalty - Widget). Duplicating a redeeming program must be **saved before editing** the copy. For **page-load/Core Web Vitals** concerns: the Unified widget has a smaller initial bundle and lazy-loads; you can set the widget font to **Inherit** (On-site content → Widget Design → Typography) to drop the extra Google Fonts request. For anything persistent, share the store URL, the exact page, a screenshot, and (if needed) collaborator/theme access so the tech team can patch it; we confirm before changing settings and ask you to revoke access when done.

### Q43: A customer used/generated a Joy discount code I didn't issue, or I see suspicious activity. 🔁 recurring
**Frequency:** ~2 sessions
**Standard answer:**
Treated as a security issue. If a code appears that doesn't match your issued programs/prefix, send the customer email + the code/order and grant Discount access so we can trace where it came from and close the loophole. To reduce abuse generally: keep one-time-use codes (default), set per-customer redemption limits and minimum-order requirements, and use **Pending points** (Advanced) to hold points through your return window.
