# Tone & Voice

Standards we set for all customer-facing communication — live chat, email, copilot responses, and any interaction with merchants.

## Core Tone

**Friendly, helpful, concise, and professional.**

We sound like a knowledgeable teammate, not a robot or a formal helpdesk.

## Standards We Expect From the Team

### Do

- Use simple, clear language — explain Shopify-specific terms when needed
- Be direct — lead with the answer, then provide details
- Show empathy — acknowledge frustration before jumping into solutions
- Be proactive — suggest useful features or next steps after resolving the issue
- Keep replies concise — 1–3 short paragraphs max
- Use step-by-step format with specific navigation paths (e.g., "Go to AI Assistant > Train AI > AI Skills")

### Don't

- Don't use overly formal or corporate language
- Don't over-apologize — one sincere apology is enough
- Don't make promises we can't keep (features, timelines, refunds)
- Don't use jargon without explanation
- Don't leave the customer waiting without an update

## Response Structure (for CS Agents & Copilots)

Every merchant-facing response should follow this pattern:

1. **Short direct answer** to the question
2. **Step-by-step instructions** with navigation paths
3. **Important notes or limitations** if applicable
4. **If unresolved** — ask one clarifying question and collect: store URL, plan, screenshots, steps to reproduce

## CS Lead Communication

As CS Lead, we also communicate:

- **With the team** — clear, direct, supportive. Give context on decisions. Coach through examples, not just rules.
- **With PM/TS** — structured escalations with full context (see [escalation-note template](../templates/escalation-note.md)). Lead with impact, not just symptoms.
- **With merchants (escalated cases)** — take over with calm authority. Acknowledge prior frustration, take ownership, provide a concrete resolution path.

## Examples

**Good (agent/copilot):** "Yes, you can connect WhatsApp to Chatty! Here's how: Go to Live Chat > Channels > WhatsApp > Connect. You'll need a Business Facebook page linked to your WhatsApp Business account."

**Bad (agent/copilot):** "Thank you for reaching out to us today. I would like to inform you that our application does indeed support WhatsApp integration. To proceed with the configuration, please navigate to the Live Chat section of the administration panel..."

**Good (CS Lead escalation):** "Hi [Merchant], I'm [Name], the CS Lead. I've reviewed your case and I understand your frustration. Here's what we're going to do to resolve this..."
