# Customer Success Leader (CSL) – Responsibilities (Liz)

This document describes Liz's responsibilities as **Customer Success Leader** for Avada Support Team (Chatty & Joy Loyalty), and how Betty (AI assistant) can support each area.

## 1. Monitor and Ensure Human CS Performance

**Scope**
- Track performance of CS agents across Chatty and Joy.
- Run regular QA reviews (e.g. monthly) of chats/emails.
- Identify strengths, gaps, and training needs.

**Examples of tasks**
- Review conversation transcripts and tickets.
- Score CS quality (tone, accuracy, process adherence, escalation correctness).
- Provide feedback and coaching to individual agents.
- Maintain or improve key CS metrics (FRT, resolution time, CSAT, review quality).

**How Betty can help**
- Analyse sample conversations and produce structured feedback:
  - What the merchant wanted
  - What the agent did well / did poorly
  - Suggested improved replies in Liz's tone
- Draft QA rubrics and scorecards for monthly reviews.
- Summarise performance data (when Liz provides exports from tools).
- Suggest individual coaching plans based on patterns in cases.

---

## 2. Train Human CS (Onboarding and Ongoing)

**Scope**
- Onboard new CS agents for Chatty and Joy.
- Continuously upskill the existing CS team.
- Use the existing Trello training board as the backbone.

**Examples of tasks**
- Define what a new CS agent must learn in week 1, week 2, and month 1.
- Turn real cases into training examples and exercises.
- Maintain training materials (guides, FAQs, macros, internal docs).
- Run or coordinate training sessions and refreshers.

**How Betty can help**
- Turn raw cases (Crisp logs, screenshots) into training content:
  - Case description
  - Questions for trainees ("How would you respond?")
  - Model answer + explanation
- Draft onboarding curriculum and checklists.
- Convert Notion/CS docs into structured playbooks and Q&A for human training.
- Write and refine macros / text shortcuts, with clear usage guidelines.

---

## 3. Monitor App Signals (Issues & Feature Requests) and Coordinate with Product

**Scope**
- Watch incoming signals about the products (Chatty & Joy):
  - Bugs / issues
  - Feature requests
  - Repeated friction points
- Coordinate with Product, TS, and Dev teams.
- Main channels: Crisp (chat), Trello (cards), Slack (internal).

**Examples of tasks**
- Tag and classify merchant conversations (bug vs feature request vs misuse).
- Create and update Trello cards for product and tech teams.
- Track progress on critical issues and key feature requests.
- Provide Product with clear patterns, not just one-off complaints.

**How Betty can help**
- Read exported conversations / Trello data and group them into themes.
- Suggest priority ordering based on impact and frequency (P0–P3 style).
- Draft clear, structured bug/feature descriptions for Trello cards.
- Help maintain shared CS process docs that describe how to handle each type of issue.

---

## 4. Handle High-Risk / Difficult Cases

**Scope**
- Step in on:
  - High-risk merchants (large impact, high revenue).
  - Angry customers, bad reviews, or threats to churn.
  - Cases that frontline CS cannot resolve alone.

**Examples of tasks**
- Join or review conversations with difficult merchants.
- Decide when to offer refunds, credits, or exceptions.
- Coordinate multi-team responses (CS + TS + PM).
- Protect agents from abuse and make sure they are not handling it alone.

**How Betty can help**
- Analyse high-risk conversations and propose response strategies.
- Draft sensitive replies in Liz's voice (especially emails and review responses).
- Turn resolved high-risk cases into playbooks and training examples.
- Suggest de-escalation language and boundaries that align with policy.

---

## 5. Refunds and Financial Goodwill Decisions

**Scope**
- Make or approve decisions around:
  - Refunds
  - Credits / goodwill
  - Billing-related escalations

**Examples of tasks**
- Evaluate context (usage, issue history, merchant size, policy).
- Decide when to strictly follow policy vs when to make exceptions.
- Communicate refund/credit decisions clearly and professionally.

**How Betty can help**
- Help define and refine refund & goodwill policies (shared across Chatty & Joy).
- Draft email templates and macros for common refund/billing scenarios.
- Review specific cases (when Liz shares details) and suggest:
  - Recommended decision (within policy)
  - Best way to communicate it

---

## 6. Shared CS Processes (Chatty & Joy)

**Scope**
- Maintain shared CS processes that apply to both apps:
  - Asking for reviews
  - Escalation flows
  - Sensitive situations (billing, data, outages)
- Ensure consistency between products and over time.

**Examples of tasks**
- Keep `shared-cs-process` documentation up to date.
- Align CS, TS, and Product on process changes.
- Use real cases to refine and validate processes.

**How Betty can help**
- Draft and refine shared CS process docs in English.
- Turn processes into Q&A training files for AI copilots (Chatty & Joy).
- Propose clarifications when current rules are ambiguous or conflict.

---

## 7. Continuous Improvement and Automation

**Scope**
- Identify opportunities to:
  - Reduce repetitive work for CS agents
  - Improve consistency via playbooks, macros, and bots
  - Use AI copilots effectively (for both agents and merchants)

**Examples of tasks**
- Decide which recurring cases should become macros or scripts.
- Decide which topics are safe for AI to handle vs must be human-only.
- Work with Product/Dev to iterate on copilot behaviour.

**How Betty can help**
- Analyse recurring cases and suggest candidates for automation.
- Write prompts, instructions, and training data for Chatty & Joy copilots.
- Design evaluation sets to test whether the AI behaves correctly.
